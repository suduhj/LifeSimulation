## Decisions

- Development logs live in `dev-logs/`.
- Project standards and planning documents live in `docs/`.
- `GPT.md` is the agent-facing entry point and must point to the current standards.
- Every development session should update the current day's log with completed work and next tasks.
- MVP world scope uses the few-but-deep model: 3 carefully designed worlds.
- The 3 MVP worlds are Cultivation World, Cthulhu Life World, and Post-Apocalyptic Wasteland.
- All worlds use age as the shared life-simulation axis.
- All worlds use yearly progression by default.
- Each world has its own secondary progression: cultivation realms, truth exposure, or survival/camp state.
- Cthulhu Life World allows the player to pursue the hidden truth, ignore it and freely pursue other life goals, resist eldritch forces, or become part of the cosmic threat.
- Cthulhu Life World has a dedicated design file for tone, route families, world-specific progression, event types, AI behavior rules, and ending families.
- World design and runtime data are separated: `.md` files explain design to humans and AI developers, while `.json` files provide stable data for the program.
- MVP world runtime folders are `worlds/cultivation/`, `worlds/cthulhu/`, and `worlds/wasteland/`.
- Core run flow starts with world selection, identity, talent draw, attribute allocation, and birth simulation, then advances through AI-paced events and player responses until an ending.
- AI can propose pacing, event count, event consequences, and world-state changes, but the game engine must validate state changes.
- Identity can be fully random or limited custom. Limited custom identity only accepts player-defined name and gender; AI generates the rest.
- Core attributes are Appearance, Intelligence, Constitution, Family Background, and Luck.
- Spirit, Charisma, and Combat are not core attributes. Stress resilience, social charm, and combat ability are derived capabilities.
- Starting attributes can be random or freely allocated. Free allocation has a base cap of 20 points, ordinary baseline is 4 points per attribute, and talent bonuses may exceed the cap.
- Single-attribute mortal range is 0-20. Starting or mortal-stage talent-enhanced attributes may reach 20-60 depending on rarity and source; cultivation realms and supernatural growth can exceed 60 according to scaling rules.
- Final attributes must preserve source layers: base allocation, identity bonus, talent bonus, growth bonus, temporary state modifier, and permanent injury modifier.
- Talent setup is draw 5, keep 3, using universal and world-specific talent pools.
- Single-talent rarity probabilities are Common 45%, Fine 28%, Rare 16%, Epic 7%, Legendary 3%, Mythic 1%.
- Talent effects can modify attributes, change routes, trigger hidden events, and add side effects.
- Talent effects can also use long-term growth multipliers, breakthrough bonuses, hidden mechanisms, and route-changing effects.
- Mythic talents are extremely rare and may change the entire run.
- 20 total points is ordinary person standard; 100 total points is the theoretical mortal comprehensive limit.
- Cultivation scaling uses order-of-magnitude realm bands from Qi Refining 100-1,000 through higher realms above 10,000,000.
- Cultivation World MVP uses Mortal, Qi Refining, Foundation Establishment, Golden Core, Nascent Soul, and Spirit Transformation.
- Formal release expands the cultivation chain with Void Refinement, Body Integration, Mahayana, Tribulation Crossing, and Ascension.
- Cultivation World supports sect, wandering cultivator, and family/clan routes, including route switching when justified by story state.
- Cultivation World-specific systems must not be treated as global assumptions unless documented as shared systems.
- The meaning of a point gain depends on the gain's percentage of the current tier width.
- Realm breakthroughs move the player character into a new numerical tier instead of adding small fixed values.
- Attributes have potential, manifested, and exposure layers.
- AI must narrate based on current manifested value, not full potential value.
- Default manifestation ratios are birth 5%-15%, early childhood 10%-30%, youth 30%-50%, adolescence 50%-80%, adulthood 80%-100%.
- Family Background usually manifests immediately, while Luck can remain hidden as event tendency.
- Exposure determines whether family, NPCs, sects, institutions, cults, corporations, or hostile forces notice abnormality.
- Mythic talents should define manifestation type: immediate manifestation, stage manifestation, conditional awakening, or hidden destiny.
- AI-generated people are NPCs. Player character and NPC terminology must stay distinct.
- NPC templates are identity anchors; ordinary NPCs must not be generated as excellent in all 5 attributes unless special conditions apply.
- Formal release should include player-customized worlds with AI-assisted structure generation.
- New requirements added during conversation should be written into the relevant docs, not left only in chat.
- Cthulhu Life World default public state is "abnormalities semi-public, truth highly sealed."
- Cthulhu Life World default abnormality publicity level is 2: strange rumors are widespread, but official sources deny the abnormal truth.
- Cthulhu official power is factional rather than purely good or evil: stability-maintenance, containment, research, compromise/surrender, and resistance can conflict.
- Cthulhu Life World formal stage direction is "all major stage archetypes", but MVP uses random city-stage selection.
- Cthulhu city-stage archetypes constrain AI generation rather than acting as fixed maps or forced plots.
- Cthulhu MVP city naming uses benchmark cities plus name pools: benchmarks provide stable tone and test targets, while name pools provide replay variety.
- Cthulhu benchmark cities remain hidden from player-facing selection in MVP.
- Cthulhu opening information is minimal by default, but high Intelligence, high Luck, special talents, high exposure, or special Family Background can reveal partial ambiguous clues.
- Cthulhu city exploration can produce both useful information and bad consequences. AI may set traps when justified, especially in high-difficulty worlds.
- Free-form action judgment uses different weights for plausibility, attributes, talents, relationships/resources, world state/exposure, and random probability depending on action type.
- Birth identity is determined by world + Family Background + talents. Legendary and Mythic talents can override ordinary identity logic.
- Gender can deeply affect route access, NPC reactions, social pressure, risks, and opportunities, depending on world rules.
- Player-facing events use pure text life-simulator format.
- Every event presents exactly 3 rich choices by default and always allows free-form input.
- Backend event data is stored as structured JSON, including event, choices, attribute changes, NPC relationship changes, state changes, progression changes, and world-state changes.
- Event text and choices must be richly written and cannot be bare one-word commands.
- Normal free-form input is handled without restating it; backend stores interpretation and judgment reason.
- Complex, high-risk, ambiguous, or world-breaking free-form input should be explained first and may require confirmation or clarification.
- Free-form input is not wish fulfillment; it is judged against character state, world state, story history, and hidden difficulty.
- Failed free-form input resolves by risk level. High-risk or extreme-risk failures can cause serious consequences, but failure may also create costly unexpected gains.
- Attribute and state changes should all be displayed after validation.
- Attribute changes display change amount, current value, source type, temporary/permanent status, and expected recovery condition when recoverable.
- Success rates should be shown fuzzily rather than as exact percentages by default.
- NPC relationship values are internal, while player-facing display uses labels and approximate values.
- NPC relationship display defaults to label plus rough range, such as "friendly (about 60-70)", while different worlds may override labels or presentation style.
- NPC relationship base dimensions are affinity, trust, fear, interest binding, and secret leverage. Worlds may add special dimensions such as karma, contamination dependence, or supply debt.
- Initial important NPCs are generated automatically from world, identity, Family Background, talents, and birth background, using or referencing the world's NPC template pool.
- Initial important NPC count defaults to 3-5 and varies by identity, Family Background, world rules, talents, and birth background.
- Players cannot manually specify important NPC relationships during normal starting setup.
- Important NPCs can be dynamically generated or promoted during story events, and must preserve identity, attributes, talents, relationship values, stance, goals, hidden information, and shared history.
- Important NPCs have personality, stance, hidden information, faction ties, relationship state, and persistent memories.
- Players can join factions and create factions when world/story rules allow it.
- Endings include overall evaluation, score, multiple tags, AI biography, attribute settlement, relationship settlement, faction settlement, world-state settlement, important choice summary, and unlocks.
- Every completed run receives a score.
- In-run AI is fate narrator, world host, event trigger, and consequence interpreter, not a pure strategy guide.
- Each life run has independent AI memory from birth to death; new runs do not inherit character memory unless a specific system allows it. Account-level achievements, codex records, unlocked talents/worlds, discovered endings, and meta progression may persist.
- Cthulhu default difficulty is slow-burn dynamic: danger and trap frequency rise or fall with truth exposure, corruption, occult contact, social normalcy, publicity level, player behavior, protection power, factions, and entity attention.
